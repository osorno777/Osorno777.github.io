DECLARATION OF TRUST

 

SECTION ONE: GENERAL PROVISIONS

 

First: On April 13, 2002, this agreement was entered into between John M. Cobin and Lesle Long Cobin, the Trustors hereof, and John M. Cobin, Lesle Long Cobin, Paul F. McClellan and Phillip J. Temple, the TRUSTEES hereof, by which the Trustees did agree to accept and to be bound to fiduciary obligation to manage the assets and business of this trust solely for the advantage and benefit of the beneficiaries hereof. This agreement is comprised of eight (8) pages.

 

Second: The TRUSTORS (also GRANTORS and SETTLORS) of this Trust are John M. Cobin and Lesle Long Cobin (whose maiden name was Lesle Dean Long), natural individuals, husband and wife, created by God as a free man and a free woman (hereinafter: "the Trustors").  The Trustors are not corporate entities or otherwise enfranchised to any state or person. The Trustors declare this Trust with express reservation of all their rights in law.

 

Third: The name of this TRUST shall be The JLK Trust (hereinafter: "the Trust" or "this Trust"). The term "JLK Cobin Family Estate Trust" is also hereby authorized for use on Trust documents, titles, and so forth as a proxy for the Trust when deemed necessary and advantageous by the Trustees.

 

Fourth: The present domicile of this Trust is set forth below and this Trust shall be construed under the laws of that jurisdiction.

 

c/o Post Office Box 25686

Greenville, South Carolina, America

[Greenville County]

 

Fifth: The Trustees shall have the power to remove the domicile to any other place that shall be deemed by the Trustees to be prudent, wise, necessary, appropriate, or desirable, and, in that event, this Trust shall be construed under the laws of that new jurisdiction.

 

Sixth: This Trust was declared by the Trustors and has been accepted by the Trustees as a pure, complex and irrevocable, inter vivos (living), family estate trust.

 

Seventh: The BENEFICIARIES of the Trust shall be John M. Cobin and Lesle Long Cobin, husband and wife, along with John M. Cobin’s six natural children Joshua Stuart Cobin, David Jonathan Cobin, Daniel Josiah Cobin, Grace Elizabeth Cobin, Matthew Stonewall Cobin, and Rachel Hope Cobin, and any other children that shall issue from the physical union of John M. Cobin and Lesle Long Cobin or be jointly adopted by them (hereinafter: "the Beneficiaries").

 

SECTION TWO: TERM OF THE TRUST

 

First: This Trust shall be established for an initial term of twenty (20) years from the effective date of this Trust. However, the Trustees (by unanimous decision) shall be and hereby are empowered to renew the Trust's operation for additional terms, each not to exceed twenty-five (25) years duration; provided, however that any such termination shall not exceed the maximum term allowed by the rule against perpetuities.

Second: As the date for the dissolution approaches, if there is no decision to renew the Trust, the Trustees shall proceed in a timely manner with the work of termination and dissolution, and shall distribute the assets of the Trust to the Beneficiaries on a prorata basis according to the units of beneficial interest which are issued to each of them.

 

SECTION THREE: DECLARATION OF AUTHORITY

 

First: This Trust shall be administered in accordance with the terms of this Declaration and agreement herein set forth and with the Trustees holding full legal title to the Trust assets (if there are multiple Trustees, in joint tenancy and not as tenants in common).

 

Second: The provisions of the Trust Instrument, together with the Resolutions of the Trustees, shall constitute the authority for operation of this Trust.  The authority herein established is possessed by the Trustees of the Trust alone, thereby leaving for determination by the courts only the question of conscientious dealing of these person(s).

 

Third: The Trustees shall have all the powers necessary to carry into effect the purposes of the Trust and shall have the authority to take any action to carry out its purpose; provided, however, that those actions shall not be inconsistent with other provisions herein set forth.

 

Fourth: Any determination of purpose of this Trust made in good faith by the Trustees shall be conclusive.  In construing the purposes of this Trust indenture, declaration, and of the Trust's resolutions, the presumption shall be in favor of the grant of powers to the Trustees.

 

Fifth: The Trustees are authorized to conduct the business of this Trust; to buy and sell the property of this Trust, to invest the funds of this Trust, and to execute contracts and instruments of all kinds on behalf of this Trust, in accordance with the powers set forth herein and not otherwise.

 

SECTION FOUR: ASSIGNMENT OF DUTIES TO THE TRUSTEES

 

First: The initial TRUSTEES of the Trust shall be John M. Cobin, Lesle Long Cobin, Paul F. McClellan and Phillip J. Temple, who shall serve without bond. The Trustees may also select other trustees, by unanimous decision, as they deem it to be necessary.

 

Second: The Trustees shall hold and manage the Trust property, having both a right and a duty to administer the Trust in accordance with the terms of the Trust, for the sole benefit of the Beneficiaries.

 

Third: It is the responsibility of the Trustees to make investment decisions, decisions affecting the disposition of real or personal property, provide for legal defense of the Trust, and pay any debts, taxes or just compensation that may come due.

 

Fourth: The Trustees shall designate one or more contingent Trustees to serve in the event all trustees shall die or incompetent simultaneously.  The Trustees shall review this selection from time to time to keep it in good order. 

 

Fifth: A Trustee may resign at his own discretion.

Sixth: If all of the Trustees and contingent Trustees should become personally incapacitated without otherwise being replaced, a court of competent jurisdiction shall on application of any Beneficiary of the Trust, appoint one or more Trustees as replacement. In selecting such a replacement(s), the court shall first seek the advice of the Trustors and of all the Beneficiaries of this Trust then living.

 

Seventh: The Trustees may be compensated in a reasonable amount commensurate with their service, especially those Trustees active in day-to-day Trust operations.

 

Eighth: By signing and acknowledging of this Agreement, the herein appointed Trustees accept and will perform all of the duties incumbent on them as trustees of this Trust. Whenever or new successor Trustees are elected or appointed for this Trust, the property of this Trust shall be vested in them as joint tenants without the need for any further act or conveyance.

 

Ninth: The Trustors shall execute and deliver to the Trustees of this Trust the necessary deeds conveying all right, title and interest in and to any real property transferred to the Trust and such other documents as may be required to transfer and convey all right, title, and interest in, and to, any stocks, bonds, mutual funds, mortgages, notes, receivables, policies of life insurance, bank accounts, and personal property which Trustors shall grant as irrevocable conveyances to the Trust hereby created.

 

Tenth: In equal exchange for the conveyances described in this instrument, the Trustees shall issue to the originating Trustors named herein above, certificates evidencing one hundred (100) units of beneficial interest (UBIs) for distribution among the Beneficiaries. 

 

Eleventh: The Trustees, on endorsement by the Trustor(s), shall transfer ownership of the units of beneficial interest to and among the aforesaid  Beneficiaries as provided in Section Five hereof. 

 

Twelfth: The Trustees shall not, except as expressly provided in this indenture, issue any units of beneficial interest beyond those originally authorized herein.

 

Thirteenth: All Trustees, as a condition of their appointment it over this Trust, shall accept control over the Trust Estate of this Trust and by their signatures to this document, or to a later Trust document authorizing their appointment, signify that they agree to perform the duties herein set forth and that they pledge their best efforts and interest to managing, preserving and protecting the assets of this Trust for the benefit of the Beneficiaries of the Trust.

 

Fourteenth: No decision of the Trustees or any disbursement of funds shall be effective or binding unless it is supported and passed by a majority of the trustees and written in the minutes or other permanent record of the Trust.  However, the Trustees may, by majority vote, confer upon one of their number or some other duly qualified person the role of Trust Manager.  The Manager shall have certain limited powers to make day to day decisions and necessary disbursements, subject to subsequent review and approval of the Trustees.  The Manager shall pay special attention to guard the Trust’s more volatile or wasting securities held for income, insurance, or capital appreciation.

 

Fifteenth: The trustees of the Trust shall elect one of their number as Executive Trustee, another as Secretary, and another as Treasurer.  If the Trustees shall consist of less than three trustees, these offices may be combined as the Trustees may decide. 

Sixteenth: The Trustees shall make provision as to the time and place for holding regular meetings of the Trust. An emergency meeting of the Board of Trustees shall be called upon the written request of the majority of the Trustees and shall take place at the time and place specified in such request.

 

Seventeenth: A majority of the Trustees shall constitute a quorum of the Board of Trustees for the conduct of its business at any regular or special meeting; but no decision shall become effective or binding unless a majority of all current trustees votes to support it.

 

Eighteenth: It shall be the duty of the Trustees of the Trust to manage the Trust property in a manner which the said Trustees shall believe to be most desirable and beneficial to the Beneficiaries.  In so doing the trustees of this Trust are authorized to perform any act which would be legal and proper for an individual, subject to the restrictions of applicable law and provisions contained in this indenture.

 

Nineteenth: The Trustees are authorized to establish and pay reasonable compensations for any person who shall perform active management, maintenance, or other duties for this Trust.

 

Twentieth: If one (or more) of the Trustees act in bad faith or in violation of these indentures or in a manner inimical to this Trust, the offending trustee shall be removed by a court of proper jurisdiction on an action brought by any Trustee or Beneficiary of this Trust.

 

Twenty-First: Neither the death nor insolvency or bankruptcy of any Trustee or holder of a certificate of beneficial interest shall in any way affect the operation or continuity of this Trust. All Trustees and Beneficiaries of this Trust reserve all their rights to Privacy, without prejudice to any of their rights.

 

Twenty-Second: The Trustees of this Trust shall be empowered, by unanimous decision, to increase or reduce the number of trustees.

 

Twenty-Third: The Trustees shall establish separate accounts (bank, brokerage, mutual fund, unit trust, etc.) for the Trust in which its funds shall be deposited, and the Trustees shall determine the authority by which current payments shall be disbursed or withdrawn from funds belonging to the Trust.

 

Twenty-Fourth: The Trust created herein is a separate and independent entity and, as such, cannot be held legally liable for any debts or obligations incurred personally as an individual by the Trustors or any Trustee or Beneficiary thereof.

 

Twenty-Fifth:  Since this Trust confers limited liability upon the Trustors, Trustees, and Beneficiaries thereto, a statement declaratory of such limited liability shall be printed clearly on all contracts made by and between the Trustees of this Trust or a representative thereof and any third party or parties to such contract.  Furthermore, this Trust shall be responsible only for its own obligations and never for those of any Trustor, Trustee, or Beneficiary.

 

Twenty-Sixth: The Trustees shall maintain well kept books, accounts and records; and shall preserve documents pertaining to special meetings or that have to do with the interest of the Trust. All such records shall thereafter and permanently be available to current and successor Trustees at all times.

 

Twenty-Seventh: The Trustees (or the Trust Manager acting under their auspices) shall upon majority vote disburse to the Beneficiaries all or any part of the income of this Trust.  However, unless dissolving the Trust, the Trustees (or the Trust Manager acting under their auspices) may only disburse to the Beneficiaries up to twenty percent (20%) of the then currently existing corpus of the Trust per year, whenever they deem such action to be appropriate.  This limitation may be increased to fifty percent (50%) per year if the Trustees unanimously perceive an urgent need to do so. When disbursements are made to the Beneficiaries under this provision, they shall always be made in direct ratio to the number of units of beneficial interest held by each of the Beneficiaries. Moreover, if the Trustors are deceased,  then no living beneficiary under the age of twenty-five (25) may receive more than one half of his interest in the Trust corpus until he attains age twenty-five (25).

 

Twenty-Eighth: The Trustees of the Trust shall be authorized, for the benefit of this Trust, to buy, sell, convey or hypothecate any asset of this Trust, to lend the money of the Trust, or to borrow funds using the assets of the Trust as collateral; and also to invest the funds of this Trust as permitted by law, providing that none of these acts shall violate any express provision of this indenture.

 

Twenty-Ninth: The Trustees of the Trust shall serve without bond; and that, as individuals, none of them shall ever be held personally responsible for the debts or obligations incurred by the Trust.

 

Thirtieth: The Trustees shall not be held personally liable or responsible, while performing their duties as Trustees under this indenture, for any investment losses resulting from no fault of their own; provided, however, that they shall be liable for removal as Trustee and suits for personal damages for any losses suffered by this Trust because of fraud, conversion of Trust property to personal use, gross neglect of duty, or breach of trust on their part.  Any Trustee or Beneficiary of this Trust may initiate such action.

 

Thirty-First: Any individual Beneficiary under this Trust may relinquish his entire beneficial interest in this Trust on any basis agreed in advance by all of the said Beneficiaries and Trustees. In that event, the said retired units of beneficial interest shall be canceled and shall never be sold or reissued.

 

Thirty-Second: No beneficiary as such, shall be empowered to control the Trustees in any way, nor to dictate management or investment policy to the Trust, nor to determine the disbursement of Trust income or corpus. However, the Trustees shall from time to time inform the Beneficiaries concerning the business of the Trust, and from time to time, upon request of the Beneficiaries, shall permit the Beneficiaries to examine the books and records of the Trust and explain to them its financial condition, operation and activities, but always at the discretion of the Trustees.

 

Thirty-Third:   This Declaration of Trust creates only an inter vivos Trust and not a corporation, partnership agency, or association.

 

SECTION FIVE: BENEFICIARY'S RIGHT OF ALIENATION AND ASSIGNMENT OF UNITS OF BENEFICIAL INTEREST

 

First: The beneficial interest in the Corpus assets or Estate of this Trust, primary or otherwise, of any beneficiary of this Trust shall not be subject to assignment, alienation, pledge, attachment or claim of creditors, and shall not be voluntarily or involuntarily alienated or encumbered by the beneficiary.

 

Second: John M. Cobin shall have forty-seven (47) units of beneficial interest in this Trust, Lesle Long Cobin shall have forty-four (44) units of beneficial interest in this Trust, Joshua Stuart Cobin shall have one (1) unit of beneficial interest in this Trust, David Jonathan Cobin shall have one (1) unit of beneficial interest in this Trust, Daniel Josiah Cobin shall have one (1) unit of beneficial interest in this Trust, Grace Elizabeth Cobin shall have one (1) unit of beneficial interest in this Trust, Matthew Stonewall Cobin shall have one (1) unit of beneficial interest in this Trust, and Rachel Hope Cobin shall have one (1) unit of beneficial interest in this Trust.  Three (3) units of beneficial interest in this Trust shall be unassigned and held by the Trustors (or the Trustees if no Trustor is living).  One of these units will be granted to each child born as a result of the physical union of John M. Cobin and Lesle Long Cobin, or any child jointly adopted by them, but none of these units may be granted to anyone else. On April 13, 2015 or, if earlier, either the date of the dissolution of this Trust or the date of the death of John M. Cobin, any unassigned units will be granted to Lesle Long Cobin, if living, otherwise the Trustees shall distribute them among the remaining beneficiaries of this Trust.

 

Third: In the event that John M. Cobin shall die before the dissolution of this Trust, the units of beneficial interest then issued to him shall immediately be canceled and then reissued.  Each of the then remaining beneficiaries other than Lesle Long Cobin shall, firstly, receive a distribution of four (4) of these reissued units and then, secondly, all of the remaining reissued units shall be distributed to Lesle Long Cobin, if living, otherwise to the then remaining beneficiaries of this Trust. 

 

Fourth: In the event that Lesle Long Cobin shall die before dissolution of this Trust, the units of beneficial interest then issued to her, along with any of the three (3) units discussed in the second paragraph of this section yet unassigned, shall immediately be canceled and then be reissued and distributed to John M. Cobin. 

 

Fifth: In the event that John M. Cobin and Lesle Long Cobin shall both die before dissolution of this Trust, the units of beneficial interest then issued to them, along with any of the three (3) units yet unassigned (as noted in the second paragraph of this section), shall immediately be canceled and then be reissued and distributed equally among the then remaining beneficiaries of this Trust.

 

Sixth: In the event that any beneficiary other than John M. Cobin or Lesle Long Cobin shall die before the dissolution of this Trust, the units of beneficial interest then issued to him shall immediately be canceled and reissued to a Trust for the benefit of his or her descendants per stirpes, unless he has no descendants, in which case his units of beneficial interest then issued to him shall immediately be canceled and reissued to the other beneficiaries of this Trust.

 

Seventh: The Trustees of this Trust shall organize any such new trusts with indentures generally identical to the indentures of this Trust and shall appoint suitable trustees for each from among the senior descendants or guardians of the minor descendants of the original Beneficiaries for whom the new Trust is created.  If no suitable trustees can be found for any one of these new trusts, the matter shall be referred to civil court for appointment of one or more suitable trustees.

 

Eighth: For the purposes of this section, children who were legally adopted by the deceased Beneficiary during his lifetime shall be included in the term: "descendants per stirpes".

 

Ninth: No part of this Trust shall be distributed to any beneficiary until he reaches the age of twenty-one (21) years.  In the event that this Trust shall be dissolved before all beneficiaries have reached the age of majority (21 years) the corpus of this Trust shall be distributed equally among the beneficiaries in proportion to the units of beneficial interest then issued to them, provided that the shares of any beneficiary of minor age (under 21 years) shall be held in Trust for his beneficial interest.  The said Trust shall be created as in the case of the demise of a beneficiary.  If such a minor beneficiary shall die before reaching majority age (21 years) his units of beneficial interest (in trust) shall be canceled and his descendants per stirpes; or, if there are no such descendants the units of beneficial interest shall not be reissued.

 

SECTION SIX: RIGHTS RESERVED BY THE TRUSTORS

 

First: The Trustors have relinquished all dominion of the assets granted to this Trust and this Trust is irrevocable by them.

 

Second: A Trustor shall have the right, subject to the acceptance of the Trustees, to grant residential property to this Trust with the reservation that he shall retain the right to occupy the premises as his home during his lifetime for so long as the property shall remain in the Trust; provided, however, that the Trustor shall in all other regards permanently relinquish dominion and control of the property to the Trustees for the benefit of the Beneficiaries of the Trust.

 

Third: The Trustors may grant and deliver to this Trust certain other personal and real property at their pleasure and convenience, subject to the approval and acceptance of the Trustees. Whenever possible and prudent, detailed inventories, account statements, and legal descriptions showing serial numbers and other distinguishing marks shall be delivered forthwith, as required to complete transfer.

 

SECTION SEVEN: THE TRUST PROPERTY

 

First: The Trustors, or any other person, may, subject to the approval and acceptance of the Trustees, at any time during the term of this Trust, add to the Trust Estate by making gifts thereto for the benefit of the then existing Beneficiaries.

 

Second: Any additional property promised by the Trustors shall become part of this Trust Estate immediately upon delivery to and acceptance by the Trustees and subject to the conditions agreed to at that time.

 

Third: The Trust may contain real estate, precious metals, jewelry, securities or other investment instruments, life or disability insurance policies, variable or fixed annuities, bank or unit trust accounts, and personal property.  Some of the Trust assets may be income-producing from time to time.  The Trust may hold a diversity of assets, according to the prudent wishes of the Trustees.

 

Fourth: The Trustors has transferred and delivered in Trust to the Trustees hereof the sum of one thousand United States dollars (US$1,000.00) as the original Estate of the Trust, receipt of which is hereby acknowledged, and which need not be held in an interest bearing account.

 

Fifth: Governmental designations and identifying numbers assigned to this Trust shall not be deemed to affect, inhibit, or transfer ownership or title to the government, its agents, or any local, state, or federal government agency.

 

SECTION EIGHT: CLOSING AND SIGNATURES

 

First: When employed in these indentures, the past, present and future tenses include each other; the masculine, feminine and neuter genders include each other; and the singular and plural numbers include each other.  Any and all redundancies within this indenture are used for the purposes of clarity and emphasis only.

 

Second: This is to witness that we, John M. Cobin, Lesle Long Cobin, Paul F. McClellan, and Phillip J. Temple have read the provisions of the JLK Trust agreement and understand the provisions therein.  By signing this document we agree to the execution and enforcement of this Trust whose terms have been heretofore stated.

 

Dated this thirteenth day of April 2002.
